    Litti Chokha

    🥗 Vegetarian | Main Course

    A traditional Bihari dish with smoky flavors and rustic charm

    🍽 Servings

    4 People

    ⏱ Preparation Time

    50 Minutes

    🔥 Difficulty

    Medium

    🥗 Category

    Vegetarian

    Litti Chokha is a famous traditional dish from Bihar and Jharkhand. Litti consists of baked wheat balls stuffed with spiced sattu, while chokha is a smoky mashed vegetable mix made from roasted tomatoes, potatoes, or eggplant. It is nutritious, flavorful, and deeply rooted in Indian food culture.

    Ingredients

    For Litti Dough

    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
    • Salt to taste
    • Water for kneading

    For Stuffing

    • 1 cup sattu (roasted gram flour)
    • 1 tsp garlic (chopped)
    • 1 tsp ginger
    • 1 tsp green chilies
    • 2 tbsp coriander leaves
    • 1 tsp lemon juice
    • Mustard oil
    • Spices (ajwain, kalonji, cumin, fennel)

    For Chokha

    • 2 tomatoes (roasted)
    • 2 potatoes (boiled)
    • 1 onion (chopped)
    • Green chilies
    • Mustard oil
    • Coriander leaves
    • Salt to taste

    Step-by-Step Instructions

    1. Prepare Dough: Mix flour, salt, and oil. Add water and knead into a soft dough.
    2. Make Stuffing: Mix sattu with spices, garlic, ginger, mustard oil, and lemon juice.
    3. Shape Litti: Make balls from dough, fill with stuffing, and seal properly.
    4. Cook Litti: Bake or roast litti until golden brown.
    5. Prepare Chokha: Mash roasted tomatoes, boiled potatoes, onion, chilies, and mustard oil.
    6. Serve: Serve hot litti with chokha and drizzle with ghee.

    Chef Tips

    • Use fresh sattu for best flavor.
    • Do not overfill litti to avoid breaking.
    • Roasting over charcoal gives authentic smoky taste.
    • Add mustard oil for traditional flavor.
    • Serve with ghee for richer taste.

    Storage Instructions

    Store leftover litti and chokha separately in airtight containers for up to 2 days. Reheat litti in oven or pan before serving.
